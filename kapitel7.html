Chapter 7: Kommst du mit? (Are you coming along?)

In this chapter, you will be introduced to different cultural events like music festivals. You'll talk about your cultural preferences, get involved in planning a party, write an invitation, and learn how to respond to one. This chapter focuses heavily on practical communication skills.

Kulturtipp (Culture Tip): "Die lange Nacht der Museen" Many German cities host a "Long Night of the Museums." On this night, dozens of museums stay open late, often until midnight or later, and one ticket grants access to all participating venues. It's a popular cultural event, perfect for exploring a city's art and history in a unique atmosphere, often with special programs, music, and food.

Phrases: Suggesting, Agreeing, and Declining

Making plans involves a back-and-forth conversation. Here are the key phrases you'll need.

FunctionGerman PhraseEnglish Translation
Etwas vorschlagen
(To suggest something)		 Gehen wir mal wieder in die Kneipe?Shall we go to the pub again?
Vielleicht am Samstag?Maybe on Saturday?
So um halb sieben?Around half past six?
Zustimmen
(To agree)		 Gute Idee!Good idea!
Ja, super!Yes, great!
Ablehnen
(To decline)		 Am Freitag kann ich leider nicht.Unfortunately, I can't on Friday.
Am Samstag habe ich keine Zeit, tut mir leid.I don't have time on Saturday, sorry.
Sprechtraining (Speaking Practice) Read this dialogue where Jan and Sebastian are making plans. Notice how they use the phrases above.
Sebastian: Hallo Jan! Mir geht’s super! Du, hast du am Wochenende Zeit? Gehen wir mal wieder in die Kneipe? Hast du Lust?
Jan: Gute Idee! Wann denn?
Sebastian: Vielleicht am Samstag...?
Jan: Hmm... am Samstagabend habe ich keine Zeit, tut mir leid. Ich gehe mit Kirsten ins Theater. Aber am Freitagabend habe ich Zeit.
Sebastian: Am Freitag kann ich leider nicht... Hmm... Und am Sonntag?
Jan: Am Nachmittag gehe ich mit Marc klettern. Aber danach können wir in die Kneipe gehen.
Sebastian: Ja, super. Dann bis Sonntag um halb sieben.

2. Grammar: Accusative Personal Pronouns

When a pronoun is the direct object of a verb (it answers "whom or what?"), it must be in the accusative case. This is crucial for sentences like "I see him" or "She invites us."

Key Grammar Rule: Accusative Pronouns The pronouns for "I", "you" (informal), and "he" change significantly. Memorize these forms.
Nominative (Subject)Accusative (Object)Example Sentence
ich (I)mich (me)Siehst du mich? (Do you see me?)
du (you)dich (you)Ich rufe dich an. (I'm calling you.)
er (he)ihn (him)Ich kenne ihn gut. (I know him well.)
sie (she)sie (her)Wir besuchen sie morgen. (We're visiting her tomorrow.)
wir (we)uns (us)Er lädt uns ein. (He is inviting us.)
ihr (you all)euch (you all)Ich sehe euch. (I see you all.)
sie/Sie (they/formal you)sie/Sie (them/formal you)Ich frage sie/Sie. (I'm asking them/you.)

3. Grammar: Separable Verbs (Trennbare Verben)

A very common feature of German is verbs with separable prefixes (like ein-, an-, mit-). In a main clause, this prefix detaches from the verb and moves to the very end of the sentence.

Key Grammar Rule: Separable Verbs
  1. The main part of the verb is conjugated and stays in Position 2.
  2. The separable prefix goes to the end of the clause.
einkaufen (to shop) → Ich kaufe heute Abend ein.
anrufen (to call) → Rufst du deine Mutter an?
mitbringen (to bring along) → Ich bringe einen Wein mit.
einladen (to invite) → Wir laden dich ein.
Exception: With a modal verb, the separable verb stays together as an infinitive at the end. → Ich kann heute Abend einkaufen.
