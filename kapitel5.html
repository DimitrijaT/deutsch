Chapter 5: Einkaufen und Essen (Shopping and Food)

In this chapter, you will focus on grocery shopping and people's eating habits. You will learn to express what you like and dislike, ask for things politely, and tell someone to do something using the imperative mood.

Kulturtipp (Culture Tip): Shopping in Germany German supermarkets (like Aldi, Lidl, Rewe, Edeka) are very efficient. Be prepared to pack your own bags quickly at the checkout. Many smaller shops, especially bakeries (Bäckereien) and butcher shops (Metzgereien), are closed on Sundays. Planning your grocery shopping for the week is a common practice.

2. At the Supermarket (Im Supermarkt)

Vocabulary: Quantities and Packaging

  • Weight: ein Kilo(gramm), ein Pfund (500g), hundert Gramm
  • Liquid: ein Liter
  • Packaging: eine Flasche (bottle), eine Dose (can), ein Glas (jar), eine Packung (package/box), ein Becher (cup/pot)

Phrases: Prices and Shopping

When asking for the price, the verb changes depending on whether the noun is singular or plural.

How to Ask "How much?"
  • Singular: Was kostet ein Brot? (How much does a loaf of bread cost?)
  • Plural: Was kosten die Tomaten? (How much do the tomatoes cost?)

Saying prices in German can be done in a few ways:

€0.50 → "Fünfzig Cent."
€1.05 → "Ein Euro fünf."
€1.80 → "Eins achtzig." or "Ein Euro achtzig."

3. The Imperative: Giving Instructions Politely

The imperative mood is used to make requests, give commands, or offer suggestions. Its form changes depending on whom you are addressing (du, ihr, or Sie).

Forming the Imperative

Key Grammar Rule: Imperative Forms
  • du (informal, singular): Use the verb stem. If the verb has a vowel change (e.g., e→i), this change applies! Example: geben → du gibst → Imperative: Gib!
  • ihr (informal, plural): Use the regular present tense form. Example: ihr gebt → Imperative: Gebt!
  • Sie (formal): Use the infinitive + Sie. Example: geben → Imperative: Geben Sie!
Verbdu-Formihr-FormSie-Form
geben (to give)Gib mir das Salz, bitte!Gebt mir das Salz, bitte!Geben Sie mir das Salz, bitte.
nehmen (to take)Nimm das letzte Stück!Nehmt das letzte Stück!Nehmen Sie das letzte Stück.
schneiden (to cut)Schneide das Brot!Schneidet das Brot!Schneiden Sie das Brot.
Sprechtraining (Speaking Practice) Read the dialogue aloud. Identify the imperative sentences.
Sandra: Christian, hol noch Wasser, bitte!
Christian: Sandra, gib mir das Salz, bitte!
Sandra: Wir haben noch Pizza. Esst noch ein Stück!

Expressing Opinions about Food

When someone offers you food, it's polite to express your opinion positively. Direct negative feedback like "Es schmeckt nicht" is often considered rude when you are a guest.

Positive ResponseNegative Response (be careful!)
Es schmeckt super! / Sehr lecker!Es schmeckt nicht.
Das ist sehr gut!Das ist nicht gut.
Mhmmm!(Usually better to say nothing or "Ich bin satt." - I'm full.)
