1. Sentence Structure (Satzbau)

Understanding where to place the verb is the most fundamental rule in German.

In a standard declarative sentence, the conjugated verb always comes in the second position.

Mein Name ist Max.

To form a question that can be answered with "yes" or "no", move the conjugated verb to the first position.

Haben Sie Kinder? (Do you have children?)

For questions asking for information (who, what, where, etc.), the question word (W-Wort) is in position 1, and the verb is in position 2.

If you start a sentence with something other than the subject (like a time or place), the verb still stays in position 2, and the subject moves to position 3.

Am Montag arbeite ich nicht. (On Monday I don't work.)

ðŸ“œ Separable Verbs (Trennbare Verben)

Many German verbs have a prefix that separates and goes to the end of the sentence. Common prefixes include: an-, auf-, ein-, mit-, ab-, zu-.

Ich rufe dich morgen an. (anrufen - to call)

Der Zug kommt um 10 Uhr an. (ankommen - to arrive)

Kommst du mit? (mitkommen - to come with)

With a modal verb, the separable verb stays together at the end.

Ich muss dich morgen anrufen.
