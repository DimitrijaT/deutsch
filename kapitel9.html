Chapter 9: Das war sehr schön (That Was Very Nice)

In this final chapter, you will learn to talk about the past using the simple past tense (Präteritum) of sein and haben. You will evaluate past leisure activities, learn to write thank-you emails, and master the possessive 's' in German.

Kulturtipp (Culture Tip): Saying "Thank You" After a Visit After visiting friends, especially for a weekend, it's a very common and polite gesture in Germany to send a short message or email the next day to say thank you. It doesn't have to be long, but a simple "Danke für das schöne Wochenende! Es war toll bei euch." (Thanks for the lovely weekend! It was great at your place.) is much appreciated and strengthens the friendship.

1. Talking about the Past: The Präteritum

To talk about things that have already happened, you need a past tense. The most common past tense for the verbs sein (to be) and haben (to have) in spoken German is the Präteritum (simple past).

Grammar: Präteritum of sein and haben

Key Grammar Rule: Forming the Präteritum The conjugation for these verbs in the past tense is irregular and must be memorized. A key pattern is that the 'ich' and 'er/sie/es' forms are always identical.
Pronounsein → war (was/were)haben → hatte (had)

Present: Heute bin ich in Sydney. Ich habe viel Spaß.
Past: Gestern war ich in Sydney. Wir hatten viel Spaß.

Reading Comprehension: An Email from Australia

Read Julia's email to her friend Lara. Notice the difference between past (gestern) and present/future (heute) events.

Hi Lara, mir geht es sehr gut! Australien ist prima! Gestern war ich mit Sam in Sydney. Das Opernhaus ist wirklich schön. Wir waren auch im Sydney Aquarium. Das war toll. Am Abend waren wir bei Sams Freunden. Wir hatten viel Spaß. Heute Vormittag fahren wir zum Strand. Wir gehen surfen. Und am Abend machen Freunde von Sam ein Barbecue. Liebe Grüße, Julia

2. Evaluating Past Experiences

Vocabulary: Giving Opinions

You can use the Präteritum of `sein` + an adjective to describe how something was.

Expressing Likes (Gefallen ausdrücken)Expressing Dislikes (Missfallen ausdrücken)
Der Urlaub war prima / super / klasse / toll!Der Urlaub war langweilig / schrecklich.
Das Wetter war sehr schön!Das Wetter war gar nicht gut.
Die Stadt ist toll.Das Freizeitangebot war nicht gut.
Ich hatte viel Spaß.Der Pool war zu klein.
Modifiers: zu and gar nicht
  • zu (too): Indicates a negative quality because it exceeds a limit. E.g., "Das Wasser war zu kalt." (The water was too cold.)
  • gar nicht (not at all): A strong negation. E.g., "Das Wetter war gar nicht gut." (The weather was not good at all.)

3. Writing Emails and Using the Possessive 's'

Structure of an Informal Email

Informal emails in German follow a simple, friendly structure.

Hauptteil (Main Body)Danke für das Wochenende! Es war sehr schön bei euch...Thanks for the weekend! It was very nice at your place...

Grammar: The Genitive 's' with Names

Key Grammar Rule: Showing Possession To show that something belongs to someone, simply add an -s to the end of the proper name. There is no apostrophe in standard German writing.
  • The camera of Caroline → Carolines Kamera.
  • The watch of Stefan → Stefans Uhr.
  • Exception: If a name already ends in -s, -x, or -z, you add an apostrophe instead. → Max' Fahrrad.
Sprechtraining (Speaking Practice) Practice forming possessives.
  • The house of Ida → Wie ist Idas Haus?
  • The wife of Markus → Wie heißt Markus' Frau?
  • The children of Thomas → Wie heißen Thomas' Kinder?
