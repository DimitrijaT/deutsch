Chapter 4: In der Stadt unterwegs (Out and About in Town)

In this chapter, we're out and about in town. You will learn about food and drink in German cafés and restaurants, and master how to order there. You will also explore telling time, making plans, and talking about what a city has to offer using the crucial accusative case.

Kulturtipp (Culture Tip): "Guten Appetit!" and Tipping Before starting a meal, it's customary to wish everyone "Guten Appetit!" (enjoy your meal). The appropriate response is "Danke, ebenfalls" or "Danke, gleichfalls" (Thanks, you too). When it comes to tipping (Trinkgeld), it's common to round up the bill or add 5-10%. You often tell the server the total amount you want to pay, e.g., if the bill is €18.50, you can say "Machen Sie 20" (Make it 20) and they will take €20.

Grammar Deep Dive: The Accusative Case

The accusative case is used for the direct object in a sentence—the person or thing that "receives" the action of the verb. You find it by asking "Whom or what?" (Wen oder was?). Many common verbs, like ordering, having, or seeing, require the accusative.

Key Grammar Rule: The Masculine Change The most important rule for the accusative case at this level is that only the masculine articles change.
  • der becomes den
  • ein becomes einen
  • kein becomes keinen
Feminine (die/eine/keine) and neuter (das/ein/kein) articles do not change from the nominative to the accusative.
CaseMasculine (Kaffee)Neuter (Bier)Feminine (Suppe)
Nominative (Subject)Der Kaffee ist heiß.Das Bier ist kalt.Die Suppe ist lecker.
Accusative (Object)Ich bestelle den Kaffee.Ich bestelle das Bier.Ich bestelle die Suppe.
Accusative (Object)Ich möchte einen Kaffee.Ich möchte ein Bier.Ich möchte eine Suppe.

Phrases for Understanding and Clarification

Sometimes you need to ask for help to understand. These phrases are extremely useful.

Sprechtraining: Polite Questions Practice saying these phrases. They show you are making an effort to understand.
  • Entschuldigung, das habe ich nicht verstanden. - Sorry, I didn't understand that.
  • Wie bitte? - Pardon? / Excuse me? (Used to ask someone to repeat themselves)
  • Können Sie das wiederholen? - Can you repeat that?
  • Können Sie das buchstabieren? - Can you spell that?
  • Bitte ein bisschen langsamer. - A little slower, please.
A: "Mein Name ist Paz."
B: "Paz? Entschuldigung, können Sie das buchstabieren?"
A: "P-A-Z."

3. City Life: "Es gibt" and More Vocabulary

Grammar: Using "Es gibt" (There is / There are)

Key Grammar Rule: "Es gibt" + Accusative The phrase es gibt is always followed by the accusative case.
In meiner Stadt gibt es einen Dom (cathedral).
Hier gibt es eine Bibliothek (library).
Gibt es hier ein gutes Restaurant?
