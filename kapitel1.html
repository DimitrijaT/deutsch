Chapter 1: Hallo, ich bin … (Hello, I am…)

In this chapter, you will take your first steps in German. You will learn how to greet people, introduce yourself, and understand and provide basic contact details.

Kulturtipp (Culture Tip): Du vs. Sie The distinction between the informal 'du' and the formal 'Sie' is one of the most important cultural aspects of German. Using the wrong one can be seen as disrespectful or overly familiar. When in doubt, always start with 'Sie'. You can switch to 'du' if the other person suggests it, often by saying "Wir können uns duzen."

Vocabulary: Introducing Yourself (Informal vs. Formal)

How you ask for someone's name depends on your relationship with them.

Informal (du)Formal (Sie)English
Wie heißt du?Wie heißen Sie?What is your name?
Wie ist dein Name?Wie ist Ihr Name?What is your name?
Common Answers:
Ich heiße Anna. / Ich bin Anna. (My name is Anna. / I am Anna.)
Mein Name ist Anna Schmidt. (My name is Anna Schmidt.)

2. Grammar: Personal Pronouns & Verb Conjugation

In German, verbs change their endings based on the subject pronoun. This is called conjugation. The verb sein (to be) is highly irregular and must be memorized.

Key Grammar Rule: Sentence Structure In simple statements and Wh-questions (who, what, where, etc.), the conjugated verb is always in the second position.
Ich wohne in Berlin. (Statement)
Woher kommst du? (Wh-Question)
Sprechtraining (Speaking Practice) Find a partner or practice out loud. Introduce yourself and ask your partner for their information.
A: Hallo! Ich heiße [Your Name]. Und wie heißt du?
B: Hallo! Ich bin [Partner's Name]. Woher kommst du?
A: Ich komme aus [Your Country]. Und du?
B: Ich komme aus [Partner's Country]. Ich spreche [Language]. Welche Sprachen sprichst du?
